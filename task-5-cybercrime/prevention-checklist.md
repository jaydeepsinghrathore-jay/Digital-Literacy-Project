# Stay Safe Online: Your 9-Step Checklist

* The UPI Golden Rule: Remember, you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone asks you to scan a QR code or enter a PIN to "get a refund" or "receive a prize," it is a scam.

* Watch Out for Fake Jobs: If you get a WhatsApp or Telegram message offering "easy money" for liking YouTube videos or rating hotels, block them immediately. These are traps to steal your savings.

* Double-Lock Your Accounts: Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on Instagram, WhatsApp, and your college email. This sends a code to your phone so hackers can't get in even if they know your password.

* Use Strong "Secret" Passwords: Don't use your name or "12345." Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols like Coffee#2026!. Never use the same password for your bank and your social media.

* Check the Website Link: Before typing your details into a college portal or exam site, check the URL. Official Indian government sites always end in .gov.in or .nic.in.

* Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Payments: Don't check your bank balance or make UPI payments using free Wi-Fi at cafes or railway stations. Use your own mobile data instead—it's much harder for hackers to "spy" on.

* Don't Overshare on Stories: Avoid posting photos of your Aadhaar card, college ID, or flight tickets on your Instagram stories. Scammers can use these details to steal your identity.

* Update Your Apps: When your phone says "System Update Available," do it! These updates fix "holes" that hackers use to break into your phone.

* Think Before You Click: If you get a message saying your SIM card will be blocked or your electricity will be cut, don't click the link. Call the official customer care number directly to check.

# How to Report a Crime

## If you lose money or are being harassed online, don't be afraid to speak up. The government has dedicated tools to help you:

* Call the Helpline: Dial 1930 immediately to report financial fraud.

* Report Online: Visit the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal
