# Social Media Etiquette Checklist


## The Do’s

Check your privacy: Make sure your "private" posts are actually private and not open to the whole world.

Look professional: Use your profile to show off your school projects, hobbies, and skills to future bosses.

Pause before posting: If you’re angry or upset, wait a few minutes before hitting "send" so you don’t regret it later.

Fact-check first: Don't share fake news or rumors. Make sure what you’re posting is actually true.

Connect with alumni: Follow people who have the jobs you want—it’s a great way to find internships.

## The Don’ts

Don't post illegal stuff: Avoid photos of drinking, drugs, or anything that could get you in trouble with your college.

Don't share your location: Never post your home address, room number, or your ID card. It’s a safety risk.

Don't be a bully: Stay out of online fights and avoid leaving mean comments. They stay on your record forever.

Don't steal work: Don’t post someone else’s art, photos, or writing and act like you made it.

Don't waste too much time: Don't let scrolling through TikTok or Instagram ruin your sleep or your grades.
